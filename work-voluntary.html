These are my work and voluntary experiences.

Pareto.AI is an AI-driven company focused on developing intelligent learning solutions. From 2024 to 2025, I worked there as a Mathematics AI Trainer and AI Training Captioner. In these roles, I crafted mathematical questions with solutions and generated detailed captions to enhance AI-generated content. This experience strengthened my problem-solving skills, as I had to create a wide range of mathematical problems, my creativity in designing new and varied questions, and my written communication skills by ensuring my explanations and captions were clear, concise, and accurate for interpretation.

The Access Project is a non-profit organisation that supports students from disadvantaged backgrounds to access top universities through tuition, coaching and in-school tutoring. Since 2021, I've been voluntarily working there as a tutor, tutoring GCSE and A-level mathematics and further mathematics. I am pleased with how my contribution has made a real impact to students, in particular, one of my students achieved a grade 9 in his GCSE mathematics exam which is the highest possible grade.

When I was studying for my A-level qualifications at Maiden Erlegh School, I worked as an assistant at homework club, it was an after-school club where students would come to do any homework given by their teachers. My responsibilities included supervising and supporting students with homework. I also volunteered at the Maths drop-in sessions where students who found certain maths problems or concepts to be challenging can ask for help. My role involved guiding them through their difficulties and ensuring they gained a better understanding resolve their problems. I stopped working and volunteering at Maiden Erlegh School after I graduated in 2020.
