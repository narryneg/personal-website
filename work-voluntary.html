These are my work and voluntary experiences.

The Access Project is a non-profit organisation that supports students from disadvantaged backgrounds to access top universities through tutition, coaching and in-school tutoring. From 2021 up to present day, I've been voluntarily working there as a tutor, tutoring GCSE and A-level mathematics and further mathematics. I am pleased with how my contribution has made a real impact to students, in particuar, one of my students achieved a grade 9 in his GCSE mathematics exam which is the highest possible grade.

I was offered a paid opportunity when I was studying for my A-levels at Maiden Erlegh School. I worked as an assistant at homework club, it was an after-school club where students would come to do any homework given by their teachers. My responsibilities included supervising and supporting students with homework. I also volunteered at the Maths drop-in sessions where students who found certain maths problems or concepts to be challenging can ask for help. My role involved guiding them through their difficulties and ensuring they gained a better understanding resolve their problems. I stopped working and volunteering at Maiden Erlegh School after I graduated in 2020.
